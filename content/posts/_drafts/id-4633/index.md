Data Platforms in Finance

Finance has some unique challenges. Trades can be complex. Their lifecycle even more so. Analytics are computationally expensive and risk is often now a realtime requirement. Add this to a sprawling landscape of fragmented, legacy architectures and it's hard to plot a course for success.

Today's technology offers a fresh perspective, but it's one that needs to be applied in a way sympathetic to the domain. Bank's are not internet companies.

This talk will look at different approaches to composing data platforms in finance. We'll reflect on successes to date and see see how today and tomorrow's technology offers some interesting new approaches to old and gnarly problems.
